# Storm's Beacon

You captain a small, fast, under-gunned expedition ship. Professor Storm is chasing an
ancient beacon out across the frontier, and it's your job to follow her leads, read what
the dead stations remember, and stay a jump ahead of whatever else is out there. This is a
mission about being **clever, not loud** — you win by knowing more and preparing better,
not by winning fair fights.

This guide explains *how to play*. It gives away no story.

---

## Getting started

From the mission browser, pick **Storm's Beacon** and open the map screen. Before you launch
you can set:

- **Start** — *New Game* to begin fresh, or *Continue* to pick up where you left off (your
  progress, credits, gear, and standing are saved between sessions).
- **Player Ships** and **Difficulty** — set to taste.

Launch, take a console (or the main screen), and you're in.

---

## The core loop

The whole mission runs on one simple rhythm:

1. **Hail Prof. Storm** for your next lead.
2. **Check the Quest screen** — she marks the lead there.
3. **Engage** to travel to it.
4. **Investigate** when you arrive.
5. **Hail Storm again** for what's next.

Repeat. When you're ever unsure what to do, the answer is almost always *hail Storm* or
*check the Quest screen*.

---

## The Quest screen (check it often)

Open the **Quests** tab on your console. This is your mission log and your map. It shows:

- **Your current objective(s)** — a title and a full description of what to do. Select a
  quest to read its details.
- **The Engage button** — with a quest selected, **Engage** fires your jump drive and
  travels you to that lead's location. This is how you get around the frontier; you don't
  fly there by hand.

Make a habit of checking the Quest screen after every jump and after every conversation —
new objectives appear here as the expedition unfolds.

### Charted Locations (your way home, and back)

Every named place you visit — home, a ruin, Eddy's Emporium — is recorded under a
**Charted Locations** entry in the Quest screen. To go back somewhere you've already been,
just **select it under Charted Locations and Engage**. That's your way home when things get
hot, and your way back to the Emporium to spend what you've earned. There's no separate
map — your quest list *is* your map, and it remembers where you've been.

---

## Talking to people (Comms)

Your contacts ride the **Ultra-Beam**. To reach them, open **Comms with no target
selected** and pick who you want:

- **Prof. Storm** — your expedition lead. Hail her for each new heading and whenever you're
  stuck.
- **Crazy Eddy** — a back-channel ship dealer. Hail him (or drop by his Emporium — see
  below) to buy ship gear.
- **Your crew** — hail the ship's own officers for a read on your situation.

You can also open Comms on a **station or ship you've selected** to talk to it directly.

---

## Getting around

Travel is done with the **jump drive**, and you trigger it from the **Quests tab's Engage
button** with a lead selected. Each Engage takes you straight to that objective's location —
so your "map" is really your quest list. A friendly bazaar station out in the marches can be
visited the same way, once it's on your quest list.

---

## Investigating

When you arrive somewhere, look for what's worth a closer look:

- **Science** — bring the **science array** to bear and **scan** the derelicts, ruins, and
  contacts you find. Scanning is how you read what places remember *and* how you recover
  salvage.
- **Comms** — hail stations, ships, and contacts; sometimes what you need is a conversation.

---

## Gear and upgrades

Two ways to improve your ship:

- **Buy from Eddy.** Hail Crazy Eddy on the Ultra-Beam, or visit his **Spaceship Emporium**
  and hail the station, to browse his deals. Spending with him and coming back builds a
  rapport that's worth having.
- **The Upgrades tab.** Anything you own — bought or built — is **activated** from the
  **Upgrades** tab, when you're on the right console for it. Activated gear is a temporary
  boost, so use it when it counts.

---

## Fabrication (crafting your own gear)

Your engineer can build expedition gear from scratch:

- **Salvage** is the raw material. You gather it by **scanning derelicts and ruins**.
- On the **Engineering** console (and Science), open the **Fabrication** tab. It lists what
  you can build and what each costs in salvage.
- **Fabricate** an item to start building it. Building **takes time** — plan ahead and stock
  up in the quiet stretches, not in the middle of trouble.
- When it finishes, the item lands in your inventory; **activate it from the Upgrades tab**
  when you need it.

---

## Staying alive

Your ship is an escort, not a warship — you are usually outgunned. Trading broadsides is a
losing game. Your real advantages are your **jump drive**, your **sensors**, and whatever
you've **prepared in advance**. When something dangerous closes in, the smart move is to
break for a jump or find another way out, not to slug it out.

---

## Quick tips

- **Check the Quest screen** after every jump and every conversation.
- **Hail Prof. Storm** whenever you're not sure what to do next.
- **Keep the sensors hot** — scan everything; it's how you learn things *and* earn salvage.
- **Prepare before you need it** — build and buy gear in calm moments so it's ready when it
  isn't calm.
- **Don't linger** where it's dangerous. Get what you came for and go.
- **Continue** from the map screen to resume a saved expedition.

Good hunting, Captain.
